Social Primer



The outbreak of the novel coronavirus, COVID-19, has been declared a pandemic by the WHO. The coronavirus spreads mainly through droplets produced by the direct close contact with coughing or sneezing of an infected person. Moreover, some people with the infection but without any serious symptoms can also spread the disease. Today, in the absence of the vaccines the control of the virus spread is done by maintaining the social distancing among the people. Social distancing is considered the best solution so far however monitoring of the people continues to be a difficult task for the governments and a frontline warrior as it is done manually. The artificial intelligence based voice enabled autonomous system is developed to provide a solution to this real-time problem. This autonomous system uses the live feeds of the CCTV cameras to monitor the social distancing between the persons and enable the voice alert to the crowd violating social distancing. Furthermore, this intelligent system is integrated with the mobile application which provides a live update on the number of persons in a particular region and the number of persons violating the social distancing. This autonomous system can be deployed in places that have a large gathering of people such as schools, colleges, temples, railway stations, shopping malls, bus stands, etc. The real-time video feed from CCTV cameras is loaded to the AI model, it separates the video into a vertical and horizontal grid. When a person is detected in the video frame the bounding box is drawn for each person. The distance between the detected persons in the frame is calculated using Euclidean distance.

Once the distance is measured, the bounding box count is made to acquire the strength of the crowd, and the number of people hasn't maintained a social distance of more than 1.5 meters. Subsequently, a voice alert is triggered to advise the crowd to maintain social distancing. Finally, the report is generated after stipulated time stating the comparison total number of persons and number of persons not following social distancing.
